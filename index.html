Bio

John Hersey is a technologist and music producer, born and based in New York City, working the seam between the two. His current project is Continuum — an AI that reads the emotion in live music and turns it into generative lighting and visuals in real time.

He spent eight years as a data scientist at Uber, Warner Music Group, and Publicis Groupe, across data engineering, paid media, and AI integration. As Hersey, he makes tech house rooted in the early-2000s sound — modern dub touches, driving funk-inspired basslines, inventive sampling. His tracks have been road-tested at Circoloco DC10, FUSE, Sonar, Hï Ibiza, and Warehouse Project; his debut EP on Berg Audio drew support from the industry's most respected selectors and sent its lead single into Beatport's Top 100.

He also works with businesses on practical AI adoption.

The Machine

Continuum

Software that listens to live music, reads its emotional and thematic shape in real time, and turns it into generative visuals and lighting.

The visual at the top of this page is the idea in miniature: a signal goes in, a feeling comes out, and the feeling drives the light. At full scale, Continuum does this for live venues — a real-time emotion model listening alongside the crowd.

Built with AI as the collaborator. The codebase written with Claude Code. The research — music-emotion recognition, model architectures — run with agents. A larger model distilled into a small local one fast enough to keep up live on stage. Agents handle the work; I focus on taste and direction.
